Longevity & Added Years

Health Age is not the same as your birthday. It reflects how old your body is functioning relative to population norms — based on how your lifestyle and chronic disease risk compare to people your age who follow evidence-based health behaviors.

The calculation draws on six domains from the evidence-based lifestyle medicine framework: nutrition quality, physical activity, sleep duration and quality, stress and mental health, avoidance of risky substances, and social connection. Each pillar is scored and weighted against published associations with biological aging markers (telomere length, inflammatory burden, cardiovascular function) and all-cause mortality risk.

Your disease risk profile across the 10 most common chronic conditions also factors in — because elevated disease risk is both a consequence of accelerated aging and a driver of further physiologic decline.

Your Health Age can be younger or older than your biological age depending on your inputs. It is an educational estimate based on population data, not a clinical measurement. Validated biomarker-based biological age tests (epigenetic clocks, telomere length assays) are available through specialty labs if you want a clinical-grade measurement.

Life Expectancy estimates are derived from CDC National Vital Statistics 2022 US life tables, adjusted for your current age (conditional life expectancy) and modified by your health age delta. The “With changes” figure reflects what the evidence suggests is achievable with your specific improvement opportunities. These are population-level estimates for educational context only — individual outcomes vary significantly.

Estimates are based on published lifestyle intervention literature. Individual results vary. These represent population-average effects, not guarantees. Speak with your physician before making major lifestyle changes.
